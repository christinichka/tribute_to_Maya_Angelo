Maya Angelou

Poet, Singer and Civil Rights Activist

Maya Angelou reciting her poem at the US President Bill Clinton's inauguration, January 20, 1993

    Here is the timeline of Maya Angelou's life:

  • 1928 - Marguerite "Maya" Annie Johnson born in St. Louis, Missouri
  • 1942 - 14 year old Maya and her family moved to Oakland, California
  • 1944 - At age 16 Maya became the first female cable car driver in San Francisco, California
  • 1945 -Angelou gave birth to her son, Clyde
  • 1951 - Angelou married Tosh Angelos
  • 1954 - After divorcing she changed her name to Maya Angelou
  • 1969 - Published her first autobiography "I Know Why the Caged Bird Sings"
  • 1993 - Angelou recited her poem "On the Pulse of the Morning" at US Presidential Inauguration for Bill Clinton
  • 2014 - Angelou received a lifetime achievement award from the Conference of Minority Transportation Officials as part of a session billed “Women Who Move the Nation.”
  • 2014 - Maya was found dead by her nurse.
